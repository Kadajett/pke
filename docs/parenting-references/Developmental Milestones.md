# Developmental Milestones

*Curated for Theo, born March 7, 2026. Track progress in [[Theo]] daily logs.*

---

## First 3 Months — Week by Week

### Week 1 (March 7–13)
- **Vision:** Can see 8–12 inches (distance to parent's face during feeding)
- **Reflexes:** Rooting, sucking, Moro (startle), grasp, stepping
- **Hearing:** Recognizes parents' voices from the womb; startles at loud sounds
- **Motor:** Movements are jerky and reflexive; hands mostly fisted
- **Social:** Prefers human faces over objects; brief eye contact during feeds

### Week 2 (March 14–20)
- **Vision:** Begins brief tracking of high-contrast objects (black/white patterns)
- **Feeding:** May become more efficient at latch; cluster feeding common
- **Alert periods:** Slightly longer alert windows (few minutes)
- **Hearing:** May quiet or still when hearing familiar voice

### Weeks 3–4
- **Vision:** Better focus; may briefly follow a face moving side to side
- **Motor:** Smoother limb movements begin replacing jerky reflexive ones
- **Social:** First "pre-social" smiles (not yet responsive — usually during sleep)
- **Head:** Brief head lifts when on tummy

### Weeks 5–6
- **Social:** **First true social smile** — responsive to faces/voices (huge milestone!)
- **Vision:** Can see farther; tracks objects past midline
- **Vocalization:** First cooing sounds ("ooh," "aah")
- **Head:** Better head control; can hold briefly at 45° during tummy time
- **Hands:** Beginning to unfist; may briefly hold a rattle placed in hand

### Weeks 7–8
- **Motor:** More purposeful movements; may swipe at dangling objects
- **Social:** Smiles readily at familiar faces; different cries for different needs
- **Vision:** Begins to notice own hands
- **Tummy time:** Can push up briefly on forearms

### Weeks 9–10
- **Social:** Laughing may begin; squealing with delight
- **Motor:** Brings hands to midline; may hold hands together
- **Vision:** Follows moving objects more smoothly
- **Head:** Steady head control when held upright

### Weeks 11–12
- **Motor:** Reaches for and bats at objects intentionally
- **Social:** Recognizes familiar people; may be wary of strangers
- **Vocalization:** Varied cooing, some consonant-like sounds
- **Head:** Head steady and upright when supported in sitting position
- **Hands:** Opens and shuts hands; brings hands to mouth deliberately

## 3–12 Months — Month by Month

### 3 Months
- Lifts head and chest during tummy time (mini push-up)
- Opens and closes hands; grasps and shakes toys
- Follows moving objects with eyes; recognizes familiar faces at a distance
- Babbles and imitates some sounds
- Enjoys playing with others; may cry when playing stops

### 4 Months
- Holds head steady unsupported
- Pushes down on legs when feet on hard surface
- May roll from tummy to back
- Reaches for toys with one hand
- Copies facial expressions (smile, frown)
- Begins to babble expressively

### 5 Months
- Rolls one direction (usually tummy → back first)
- Transfers objects between hands
- Interested in mirror reflections
- Responds to own name

### 6 Months
- **Sits with support** → progressing to sitting independently
- Rolls both directions
- Rocks on hands and knees (pre-crawling)
- Rakes at small objects (not yet pincer grasp)
- **Stranger anxiety** may begin
- Ready for **solid food introduction** (see pediatrician)
- Babbles chains of consonants ("babababa")

### 7 Months
- Sits without support
- Supports full weight on legs when held standing
- Transfers objects hand to hand
- Explores objects by banging, shaking, throwing
- Responds to "no" (understands tone)

### 8 Months
- May begin crawling (or scooting, army crawling — all valid)
- Pulls to stand holding furniture
- **Pincer grasp developing** (thumb + forefinger)
- Object permanence developing — plays peek-a-boo
- **Separation anxiety peaks** — see [[Sleep Science for Infants#8-Month Regression]]

### 9 Months
- Crawls well; may cruise along furniture
- Points at objects
- Understands "no"
- Plays pat-a-cake, waves bye-bye
- Pincer grasp refined — picks up small foods

### 10 Months
- Stands briefly without support
- Uses objects correctly (drinks from cup, brushes hair)
- Says "mama" or "dada" with meaning
- Explores cause and effect (drops things to watch them fall)

### 11 Months
- Cruises confidently
- May stand alone for several seconds
- Follows simple one-step commands ("give me the ball")
- Shows preferences for certain people and toys

### 12 Months
- **May take first steps** (range: 9–16 months is all normal)
- Says 1–3 words with meaning
- Imitates actions (stirs with spoon, "talks" on phone)
- Shows attachment to specific caregivers
- Understands simple instructions
- **Points to communicate** wants and interests

## Sensory Development

### Vision
| Age | Capability |
|-----|-----------|
| Birth | Sees 8–12 inches; prefers high contrast, faces |
| 1 month | Follows objects briefly to midline |
| 2 months | Follows past midline; beginning color vision |
| 3 months | Recognizes faces across room; full color vision |
| 4 months | Depth perception developing |
| 6 months | Near-adult visual acuity for color and clarity |

### Hearing
- Fully developed at birth
- Startles to loud sounds (Moro reflex)
- Prefers human voice, especially higher pitch
- By 4–6 months: turns to locate sounds
- By 9 months: understands several common words

### Touch
- Most developed sense at birth
- **Skin-to-skin** promotes regulation (see [[Newborn Care Essentials (0-3 Months)#Skin-to-Skin Contact]])
- Mouthing objects is normal exploration (not just teething) starting ~3–4 months

## Tummy Time

Essential for motor development, head/neck strength, and preventing flat head (positional plagiocephaly).

### Guidelines by Age
| Age | Duration | Frequency |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| 0–2 weeks | 1–2 min | 2–3x/day (after every diaper change is easy to remember) |
| 2–4 weeks | 3–5 min | 3–4x/day |
| 1–2 months | 5–10 min | Multiple times/day; total 15–30 min |
| 2–3 months | 10–15 min | Work toward 30–60 min total/day |
| 3–4 months | 15–20 min | 60+ min total/day |

### Tips
- Chest-to-chest on a parent counts as tummy time
- Use a rolled towel under chest/armpits for support early on
- Get on the floor at baby's level — make it social
- Stop if baby is distressed; try again later
- Tummy time on a Boppy or across a lap also works

### Milestones During Tummy Time
- **2 weeks:** Turns head side to side
- **1 month:** Brief head lift (45°)
- **2 months:** Head up at 45° for sustained periods; pushes up on forearms
- **3 months:** Mini push-up (chest off floor); weight on forearms
- **4 months:** Pushes up on hands; may reach for toys
- **5–6 months:** Pivots in a circle; rocks on hands and knees

## When to Be Concerned

These are not "your baby is delayed" flags — they're "mention this to your pediatrician" markers:

### By 2 Months
- Doesn't respond to loud sounds
- Doesn't follow moving things with eyes
- Doesn't bring hands to mouth
- Can't hold head up when pushing up on tummy

### By 4 Months
- Doesn't watch things as they move
- Doesn't smile at people
- Can't hold head steady
- Doesn't coo or make sounds
- Doesn't bring things to mouth
- Doesn't push down with legs when feet on hard surface

### By 6 Months
- Doesn't reach for things
- Shows no affection for caregivers
- Doesn't respond to sounds around them
- Difficulty getting things to mouth
- Doesn't make vowel sounds
- Doesn't roll in either direction
- Seems unusually stiff or floppy

> **Source:** CDC "Learn the Signs. Act Early." milestones checklist (updated 2022). WHO Motor Development Study, 2006. Dosman CF et al. "Evidence-based milestone ages as a framework for developmental surveillance." *Paediatrics & Child Health* 17(10), 2012.
